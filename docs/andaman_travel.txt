Travel Guide: The Andaman Islands

The Andaman Islands are an archipelago in the Bay of Bengal, administered as part of the Indian union territory of Andaman and Nicobar Islands. Known for white sand beaches, clear turquoise water, and some of the best preserved coral reefs in India, they have become a popular destination for honeymooners and diving enthusiasts.

Port Blair, the capital, is the entry point for nearly all visitors, connected by direct flights from Chennai, Kolkata, Bengaluru, and Delhi. The city's most visited site is the Cellular Jail, a colonial-era prison where Indian freedom fighters were incarcerated; the evening sound and light show narrates its history. Most travelers spend only a day in Port Blair before heading to the islands.

Havelock Island, officially renamed Swaraj Dweep, is the most popular island. Radhanagar Beach on its western coast is regularly ranked among the best beaches in Asia, with a long stretch of fine white sand backed by forest and a spectacular sunset view. Elephant Beach offers snorkeling over shallow reefs, and the island is the main hub for scuba diving, with dive schools offering beginner courses and fun dives at sites like the Wall and Aquarium.

Neil Island, renamed Shaheed Dweep, is smaller and quieter, known for its natural rock bridge, laid-back pace, and beaches named Bharatpur, Laxmanpur, and Sitapur. Many travelers split their time between Havelock for activity and Neil for relaxation.

Travel between islands is by ferry. Private catamarans like Makruzz and Nautika are faster and air-conditioned, while government ferries are cheaper but slower and harder to book. Ferry tickets sell out in peak season, so booking in advance is strongly recommended.

The best time to visit is from October to May, when seas are calm and skies are clear. The monsoon months from June to September bring rough seas, ferry cancellations, and suspended water sports. A trip of five to six days comfortably covers Port Blair, Havelock, and Neil.

Indian citizens do not need a permit for the main tourist islands, while foreign nationals receive a free Restricted Area Permit on arrival. Mobile network coverage is limited and internet is slow across the islands, which many visitors come to consider a feature rather than a drawback.
